\begin{abstract}
    由于传统有限元分析在求解弹性波动力问题时，
    普遍倾向于采用空间域弱形式离散而保留时间域的强形式差分逐步求解(如经典的Newmark-$\beta$法)。
    这种半离散方法在结构面临长时间持续响应、复杂多维相互作用及高度演化计算时，
    往往受制于时间步长所积累的误差及格式的不稳定性。鉴于此，
    本文提出并系统研究了一种基于完全弱形式的时空混合有限元分析框架。
    该架构打破了空间与时间异构离散的常规思路，
    实现了真正意义上针对整个时空连续体的联合一致性变分离散，
    为高维弹性波问题的数值分析提供了一套理论完备、
    结构对称且高度稳定的全新途径。

    首先，本文从传统哈密顿变分原理出发，
    指出了其在进行时间弱形式离散时由于必须强制设定时间终点虚位移为零($\delta u(t_1)=0$)而产生的根本性计算阻碍。
    针对该矛盾，本文创新性地引入了独立的对偶伴随变量$v(x,t)$，
    重构出包含双变量的拉格朗日乘子型混合变分能量泛函，
    直接规避了无穷时间端点上的极值限制。

    其次，对于由端部状态自由化所引致的大型系统刚度矩阵奇异性痛点，
    提出并设计了具有针对性调整能力的边界控制罚函数体系。
    研究推演了在“1+1”维及“2+1”维(时间+空间)波动模型中，
    通过附加虚应力刚度项来填补系统边值矩阵缺陷的底层机制，
    建立起无截断且具备极高架构对称性的封闭微分算阵。

    最后，充分运用多维格林公式、分阶空间导数运算与全域分部积分，
    不仅实现了这一时空双参数拉格朗日模型与其底层的“2+1”高维强物理偏微控制方程的完美等价性数学论证；
    还通过详尽的数值分析与结构验证，充分展示了该完全弱形式算法平台在压制数值色散、
    贴合时空动态相容边界与维护长效演化的高保真度计算上的优良综合性能。
    为后续将其拓展至真正的大规模四维空间及多物理场复杂系统评估奠定了稳固的基础。

\end{abstract}

\begin{abstractEn}
    When solving elastodynamic wave problems, traditional finite element analysis generally prefers spatial weak-form discretization while retaining temporal strong-form finite difference step-by-step integration (such as the classic Newmark-$\beta$ method). When dealing with long-term continuous responses, complex multi-dimensional interactions, and highly evolutionary computations, this semi-discretization approach is often constrained by error accumulation from time steps and the instability of the scheme. In view of this, this thesis proposes and systematically investigates a space-time mixed finite element analysis framework based on a full weak form. This architecture breaks the conventional paradigm of heterogenous spatial and temporal discretizations, achieves a truly unified and consistent variational discretization for the whole space-time continuum, and provides a novel, theoretically complete, structurally symmetric, and highly stable approach for the numerical analysis of high-dimensional elastic wave problems.

    First, starting from the traditional Hamilton's variational principle, this thesis identifies the fundamental computational obstacle caused by the mandatory requirement of zero virtual displacement at the final time ($\delta u(t_1)=0$) during temporal weak-form discretization. To resolve this contradiction, an independent dual adjoint variable $v(x,t)$ is innovatively introduced to reconstruct a Lagrange multiplier-type mixed variational energy functional with dual variables, successfully circumventing the extreme constraints at the infinite time endpoints.

    Second, to address the singularity issue of the large-scale system stiffness matrix caused by the liberalization of endpoint states, a boundary control penalty function system with targeted adjustment capabilities is proposed and designed. The underlying mechanisms of repairing the defects of the system's boundary value matrix by appending virtual stress stiffness terms in both "1+1" and "2+1" dimensional (space+time) wave models are derived, establishing a closed differential operator matrix without truncation and with extremely high architectural symmetry.

    Finally, by fully utilizing the multi-dimensional Green's formula, step-by-step spatial derivative operations, and global integration by parts, this study not only mathematically proves the perfect equivalence between the space-time dual-parameter Lagrangian model and its underlying "2+1" high-dimensional strong-form physical partial differential governing equations, but also comprehensively demonstrates the excellent overall performance of the full weak-form algorithm platform in suppressing numerical dispersion, fitting space-time dynamic compatible boundaries, and maintaining high-fidelity computations for long-term evolution through exhaustive numerical analysis and structural verifications. This lays a solid foundation for its future extension to authentic large-scale four-dimensional spaces and the evaluation of multi-physics complex systems.
\end{abstractEn}
\keywordsEn{Space-time mixed finite element; Wave dynamics; Full weak form; Lagrange multiplier method; Penalty function method}
